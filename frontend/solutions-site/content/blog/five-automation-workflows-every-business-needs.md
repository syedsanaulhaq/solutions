# 5 Business Processes You Should Automate This Year

## Why automation projects fail

Most automation initiatives start with the wrong question. Teams ask "what is the most advanced thing we could automate?" when they should be asking "what repetitive task is costing us the most time **right now**?"

The best automation projects are not the most technically impressive — they are the ones that consistently save 10–20 hours of skilled staff time per week by removing work that should never have been manual.

Here are five processes that almost every growing business handles manually today, and that consistently deliver fast ROI when automated.

---

## 1. New client onboarding

**Time cost without automation:** 45–90 minutes per new client

When a deal closes, what happens next? In most companies, someone from operations manually:
- Creates accounts in multiple systems
- Sends the welcome email
- Schedules the kick-off call
- Adds the client to Slack

...often from a checklist in a Notion doc.

**What automation delivers:**
A well-built onboarding automation triggers all of this from a single CRM event. New accounts get created, the welcome sequence fires, the Slack notification goes out, and the account manager's task appears in their project management tool — **all within 90 seconds** of the contract being marked signed.

---

## 2. Invoice and document generation

**Time cost without automation:** 2–4 hours per week for project-based businesses

For project-based businesses, invoice generation is often a manual process: copy data from the time tracker into the invoice template, check the rates, send for approval. Mistakes happen. It takes time. And someone has to remember to do it.

**What automation delivers:**
The automation generates the document directly from your time-tracking data, pre-fills all the correct data, and either sends it automatically or presents it for **one-click review and approval**. Error rate drops to near zero.

---

## 3. Lead routing and CRM enrichment

**Time cost without automation:** 15–30 minutes per inbound lead

When a lead comes in through your website, chatbot, or inbound email, how long does it take to reach the right salesperson? And how complete is the CRM record when they receive it?

**What automation delivers:**
A routing automation:
1. Enriches the lead with company data from Clearbit or Apollo
2. Scores it against your ideal customer profile
3. Routes it to the appropriate team member
4. Sends the contact a personalised acknowledgement

...all before a human has read the first notification. Average response time drops from hours to seconds.

---

## 4. Recurring report delivery

**Time cost without automation:** 1–3 hours per report run

Every business has reports that someone generates manually each week or month: the performance dashboard pulled together in Excel, the client-facing progress report assembled from three different tools.

This is often work that **senior people** do because they know how to navigate the systems — which means it costs even more than it appears to.

**What automation delivers:**
A scheduled reporting automation pulls data from connected systems, formats it into a consistent template, and delivers it to the right people at the right time. No manual assembly, no missed weeks, no formatting inconsistencies.

---

## 5. Subscription renewal and churn prevention

**Time cost without automation:** Often invisible — until customers churn silently

For subscription businesses, the most valuable automation is often the one that identifies at-risk customers **before they churn**. Usage data, login frequency, support ticket volume, and payment failure signals can be combined into a risk score that triggers the right intervention.

**What automation delivers:**
A triggered sequence that fires based on risk signals — a check-in from customer success, an automated re-engagement offer, or an escalation to the account manager — **before the cancellation happens**.

---

## Where to start

Pick one process, build it properly with error handling and alerting, measure the time saving, and use that success to build the case for the next one. The compound effect of automating five processes over 18 months is transformative.
