## Thread 2: Automation ROI (educational, share-worthy)

---

**Tweet 1:**
How to calculate whether automating a business process is worth it.

A simple framework: 🧵

---

**Tweet 2:**
Step 1: Calculate the current cost

Hours/week × fully-loaded hourly rate × 48 weeks

Example:
3hrs/week × £43.75/hr × 48 = £6,300/year

That's the cost of doing nothing.

---

**Tweet 3:**
Step 2: Estimate the automation cost

Custom build: £3,000–£15,000
Running costs: £100–£500/month
Annual maintenance: 15-20% of build

---

**Tweet 4:**
Step 3: Calculate payback

Net annual saving = process cost − running costs
Payback period = build cost ÷ net saving

Under 18 months = strong investment
2-3 years = reasonable
3+ years = scrutinise the assumptions

---

**Tweet 5:**
The best automation candidates:
→ High repetition, low variation
→ Clear, consistent business rules
→ Measurable inputs and outputs

Avoid automating:
→ Processes that require judgement every time
→ Processes with unclear or inconsistent rules
→ Broken processes (you'll just scale the mistakes)

---

**Tweet 6:**
The 3 automation traps:

1. Over-engineering (2hr/week task doesn't need microservices)
2. Automating a broken process (automation scales mistakes)
3. No error handling (silent failures for weeks)

Most automation projects fail on #3.
