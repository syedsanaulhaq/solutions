## Post 2 (Technical Audience — 3 days after launch)

---

**Building something custom vs buying off-the-shelf: a framework**

We've been asked this question a hundred times this month, so here's the short version:

Off-the-shelf wins when:
→ Your requirements match what the product was designed for
→ You can afford the SaaS pricing at scale
→ You don't need deep integrations with your existing stack

Custom wins when:
→ You're selling the software as part of your product
→ Your compliance or data residency requirements aren't met by vendor infrastructure
→ The long-term SaaS cost exceeds the build cost over 4–5 years
→ You need capabilities the platform will never build

We just published a full breakdown on each of our three core areas — LMS, AI chatbots, and automation — with cost models and decision frameworks.
